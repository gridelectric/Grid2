# Phase 2: Mobilization Authorization

## ðŸ”¹ What Triggers This Phase

Phase 2 begins when [[Grid Electric]] receives verbal authorization to mobilize from the customer.

This typically includes:

1. A phone call or email from the client confirming they are authorizing mobilization.
2. [[Jeanie]] prepares and sends the [[NTP]] (Notice to Proceed) to the customer for formal signature or signs the customers contract.

> [!info] What is a Notice to Proceed (NTP)?
> 
> The NTP is a formal document created by [[Grid Electric]] that includes:
> 
> 1. Billing terms and storm rates
> 2. Authorized expense details
> 3. Expected arrival times and mobilization commitments
> 4. Points of contact and response requirements

> [!warning] Deployment does not begin until Jeanie receives written authorization back from the client. In some cases, a clearly written confirmation (email or text) may be accepted if timing is critical. This decision is at [[Jeanie]]'s discretion and must be documented.

> [!danger] Storm Deployment is High Pressure
> 
> This phase moves quickly and under pressure. The customer may request changes on short notice, personnel may drop out unexpectedly, and mistakes can be costly.
> 
> ðŸ›‘ **Document everything.** Save all email confirmations, screenshot text approvals, and record verbal commitments in writing. Admins must protect the company by maintaining accurate records and providing timely updates.

> [!clock] Resource Planning Reminder
> 
> Initial resource estimation happens in [[Phase 1]]. [[Jeanie]]:
> 
> 1. Reviews [[MS Forms]] signups
> 2. Sorts by travel zone and availability
> 3. Responds to the customer with approximate crew counts
> 
> Phase 2 is about locking in the teams, confirming availability, and deploying.
